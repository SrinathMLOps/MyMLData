Understanding Pearson's Correlation Coefficient (r):
Range of r:
r = 1: Perfect positive correlation.
r = -1: Perfect negative correlation.
r = 0: No correlation (the variables are unrelated linearly).
Scatterplot Interpretations in a Single Line:
r = -0.9: Strong negative linear relationship, where y decreases as x increases.
r = -0.5: Moderate negative correlation, showing a downward trend but with some variability.
r = 0.0: No correlation, as thereâ€™s no visible relationship between x and y.
r = 0.5: Moderate positive correlation, with y generally increasing as x increases.
r = 0.9: Strong positive linear relationship, with y increasing as x increases.
r = 0.99: Almost perfect positive correlation, where y increases almost exactly in line with x.

install.packages("ISLR")
library(tidyverse)  
library(ISLR)       
credit = as_tibble(Credit)  
credit = select(credit, Balance, Limit, Income) 
summary(credit)

ggplot(credit, aes(x=Balance, y=Limit)) +
  geom_point()

ggplot(credit, aes(x=Balance, y=Income)) +
  geom_point()

ggplot(credit, aes(x=Income, y=Limit)) +
  geom_point()

cor(credit$Balance, credit$Income)

cor(credit$Income, credit$Limit)

install.packages("gridExtra")
library(gridExtra)
plot1 <- ggplot(credit, aes(x=Income, y=Limit)) +
  geom_point() +
  ggtitle("Income vs Limit")
plot2 <- ggplot(credit, aes(x=Balance, y=Income)) +
  geom_point() +
  plot3 <- ggplot(credit, aes(x=Balance, y=Limit)) +
  geom_point()
  ggtitle("Balance vs Income")
gridExtra::grid.arrange(plot1, plot2,plot3, ncol = 3)
